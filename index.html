Graphical User Authentication (GUA)

Graphical User Authentication (GUA) is a security method where users identify or authenticate themselves by interacting with images or visual elements, rather than using traditional text-based passwords. The idea is that users are more likely to remember images, patterns, or specific locations within an image than a string of alphanumeric characters. GUA systems can take various forms, such as choosing a sequence of images, drawing a pattern, or clicking on specific points in an image. These systems aim to offer a user-friendly yet secure alternative to traditional passwords, addressing issues like password memorability and resistance to certain types of attacks, such as brute force or dictionary attacks..
